How to get involved:

Volunteers should be reliable and committed, caring and have a passion for helping people. In addition, all volunteers must be:

  • Committed to fulfilling at least a one year commitment.
  • Committed and available to complete a minimum of 4 hours per week, each week, for a minimum of one year
  • Up to date on all vaccinations and have completed and pass a Tspot blood test.
  • Available to receive the required seasonal flu vaccine.
  • Able to complete and pass a criminal background check.
  • Note:A program for high school and college students is held each summer for a 2 month long period. The Student program is closed for 2019.

Auxiliary Scholarships

The ADVANCED SKILLS SCHOLARSHIP is available to full-time employees of Emory Johns Creek Hospital who meet certain criteria established by the Auxiliary. Our goal is to enhance the opportunities for our employee’s careers at Emory Johns Creek Hospital. If you are interested in applying for the Advanced Skills Scholarship, speak to your supervisor. The Auxiliary has a limited number of scholarships of up to $1500 each. The awarding of scholarships will be at the discretion of the Emory Johns Creek Hospital Auxiliary.

  • A completed application
  • Two (2) signed letters of recommendation on appropriate letterhead and in a sealed envelope. One should be from a teacher or supervisor
  • A short narrative essay stating your career goals and reason you chose a health-related field of study
  • Official transcript from the last educational institution attended
  • Verification letter from school indicating acceptance into a health-related program, including date of acceptance (month and year) and proposed date of graduation (month and year) We cannot accept applications for pre-med or pre-nursing. Physical therapy and occupational therapy applicants must furnish letter of acceptance to a master's program.
  • Completed and signed application and agreement
  • Must be received no later than April 12, 2019 with notification by June 1, 2019.

Applications can be downloaded by clicking on the Auxiliary Scholarship Navigation tab. Please be sure to read the application carefully for exact requirements.

Application Instructions

Completed applications with all required attachments must be received by the Scholarship Committee on or before April 12, 2019. Scholarship recipients will be selected by the Committee and notified, in writing of their selection, on or before June 1, 2019. All selections by the Committee will be considered final. Scholarship money awarded by the Auxiliary can be used to include tuition, lab fees, and registration. It can also be applied to the cost of books, special fees, special equipment needed, and uniforms that are necessary in the course of study. Scholarship money will be sent directly to the school. Scholarship funds cannot be used for room and board.

A member of the Scholarship Committee will be more than happy to answer any questions you may have regarding the scholarship program. Please direct questions to: Eileen Kovalchik, Scholarship Chairperson, at 770-442-5390.

We would be happy to consider the application of a student who meets the criteria. It would be our pleasure and privilege to provide some financial support in your educational pursuit.

To apply, download the following scholarship documents.Specific instructions for completing and submitting applications can be found in the "Checklist" portion of the application.

Advanced Skills Scholarship

The ADVANCED SKILLS SCHOLARSHIP is available to full-time employees of Emory Johns Creek Hospital who meet certain criteria established by the Auxiliary. Our goal is to enhance the opportunities for our employee’s careers at Emory Johns Creek Hospital. If you are interested in applying for the Advanced Skills Scholarship, speak to your supervisor. The Auxiliary has a limited number of scholarships of up to $1500 each. The awarding of scholarships will be at the discretion of the Emory Johns Creek Hospital Auxiliary.

ADVANCED SKILLS GUIDELINES

  • Immediate supervisor/department head to write a letter stating that this course will be beneficial for applicant to take (letter must be attached to the scholarship application.)
  • Applicant must write an essay indicating how the Advanced Skills Scholarship is going to advance their career at Emory Johns Creek Hospital within their department.
  • Applicant must be a full-time employee of Emory Johns Creek Hospital.
  • Proof of registration for course is required.
  • Applicant will complete and submit application (with required letters attached.)
  • Payment will be made directly to the school/organization upon receipt of proper documentation.
  • The scholarship can be used only for course registration fees, tuition and books.

Anyone interested in applying for this scholarship should contact the auxiliary scholarship chairperson (at the address listed below). Auxiliary approval, in writing, will be provided to the applicant.

ELIZABETH WILMOT BULL SCHOLARSHIP

The ELIZABETH WILMOT BULL SCHOLARSHIP is co-sponsored by the Georgia Council on Auxiliaries/Volunteers of the Georgia Hospital Association. This scholarship is awarded to a student pursuing a health-related degree.

Qualifications for this scholarship include:

  • Enrolled in a medical/health-related course of study in a Georgia college. Pre-med and pre-nursing applications cannot be accepted. Programs such as biology or chemistry as stand alone majors or on-line programs or med techs do not qualify, nor does Veterinary Medicine.
  • Two (2) letters of reference from teacher, counselor, supervisor, or clergy on appropriate letterhead, dated and in a sealed envelope.
  • A 3.0 GPA or better
  • Narrative profile of yourself, stressing factors relevant to your occupational choice and goals.
  • Official high school and/or college transcript and available aptitude and achievement tests. High school transcript needed only if you are entering your freshman year of a medically-related program through a technical school.
  • Official proof of acceptance (if not currently enrolled) from the school you will attend.
  • A personal interview with a letter of recommendation from the President or Scholarship Chairperson of the sponsoring Auxiliary is required. Letter of recommendation must be on Auxiliary letterhead, dated and signed.
  • Completed application form with all the reqired attachments must be postmarked to the Elizabeth Wilmot Bull Scholarship Chairperson not later than January 31, 2019
  • Applications are available at the Concierge's Desk of the Emory Johns Creek Hospital during regular business hours or by contacting Eileen Kovalchik at emklib@bellsouth.net. Please read the application carefully for exact requirements.
